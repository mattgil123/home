Property Tax Appeals

Overassessed property taxes can strain family budgets—especially during divorce, relocation, or estate transitions. At O’Keefe, we aggressively fight for reductions in Cook County’s southwestern suburbs, leveraging our deep local knowledge to safeguard your assets and deliver real savings.

Our practice focuses exclusively on these neighborhoods for unmatched expertise, avoiding the pitfalls of broad Cook County coverage. We analyze market data, income, sales, vacancy rates, and more to challenge unfair assessments—helping you secure exemptions, freezes, or certificates of error you might otherwise miss.

Residential Properties

We represent homeowners at every appeal level, reviewing your assessed value annually (even on Cook County's 3-year cycle). Special division for properties over $600,000—ensuring fair taxation for single-family homes and rentals alike.

Commercial Properties

Aggressive challenges to Assessor’s Offices and Boards of Review, with readiness to escalate to Circuit Courts or the Illinois Property Tax Appeal Board. We monitor yearly for market-driven reductions, protecting your business bottom line.

Condos & Townhomes

Joint appeals for associations yield big savings per unit. We handle shared factors like ownership percentages and sales data, representing boards before assessors, courts, and appeal boards for maximum impact.

Our Property Tax Appeal Process

  1. Free Assessment Review – We evaluate your property's data and identify reduction opportunities.
  2. File Complaint – Submit evidence to local Assessor’s Offices and prepare for hearings.
  3. Negotiate & Appeal – Advocate aggressively; escalate to courts or PTAB if needed.

  5. Ongoing Support – Annual reviews to catch new exemptions or market shifts.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does divorce take in Illinois?

Uncontested: 1–3 months. Contested: 6–18+ months depending on complexity and court schedule.

Do I need a lawyer for an uncontested divorce?

Not required, but highly recommended to avoid costly mistakes in asset division or support agreements.

Can custody agreements be changed later?

Yes — with proof of “significant change in circumstances” (e.g., relocation, income change, safety concerns).

What is “equitable distribution” in Illinois?

Marital property is divided fairly (not always 50/50), based on contributions, needs, and duration of marriage.

How often are properties reassessed in Cook County?

Most residential properties every 3 years, but we review annually for opportunities like market dips or exemptions.

Can I appeal my property taxes if I'm in a divorce?

Absolutely—reducing assessments can protect your share of marital assets. Contact us for integrated family law and tax strategy.
